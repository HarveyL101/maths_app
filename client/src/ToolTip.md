# Info to be fed into the ToolTip system

## Number

### Number & Place Value

**Year 3:**
Pupils work with three-digit numbers. They count in specific multiples (4, 8, 50, 100) and find 10 or 100 more or less. They recognise hundreds, tens and ones, compare and order numbers to 1000, and read and write numbers in words and numerals.

**Year 4**:
Pupils extend to four-digit numbers and count in new multiples (6, 7, 9, 25, 1000). They round to the nearest 10, 100 and 1000, work with negative numbers, and read Roman numerals to 100.

**Year 5**:
Pupils work with numbers up to 1,000,000. They count in powers of 10, interpret negative numbers across zero, and round up to 100,000. They read Roman numerals to 1000.

**Year 6**:
Pupils extend to numbers up to 10,000,000. They round to a required degree of accuracy and solve contextual problems involving negative numbers.

### Addition & Subtraction

**Year 3**:
Pupils add and subtract three-digit numbers mentally and using formal column methods. They estimate answers and check using inverse operations. They solve missing number and word problems.

**Year 4**:
Pupils use formal written methods with four-digit numbers. They solve two-step problems and decide which methods are appropriate.

**Year 5**:
Pupils add and subtract numbers with more than four digits using formal and mental methods. They solve multi-step contextual problems and use rounding to judge accuracy.

**Year 6**:
Pupils solve multi-step problems involving all four operations. They estimate to check answers and justify their method choices.

### Multiplication & Division

**Year 3**:
Pupils recall the 3, 4 and 8 times tables. They multiply two-digit numbers by one-digit numbers and solve simple scaling and correspondence problems.

**Year 4**:
Pupils recall all multiplication facts to 12 × 12. They multiply two- and three-digit numbers by one digit using formal methods and use factor pairs and the distributive law.

**Year 5**:
Pupils multiply up to four digits by one- or two-digit numbers using long multiplication. They divide using short division and interpret remainders in context. They identify primes, factors and multiples.

**Year 6**:
Pupils divide using long division with two-digit divisors. They apply order of operations and solve complex multi-step problems involving all four operations.

### Fractions (Including Decimals & Percentages)

**Year 3**:
Pupils recognise and use unit and non-unit fractions. They work with tenths and add and subtract fractions with the same denominator.

**Year 4**:
Pupils extend to hundredths and decimal equivalents of common fractions. They compare decimals and solve simple problems involving fractions and money.

**Year 5**:
Pupils convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions. They add and subtract fractions with related denominators and multiply fractions by whole numbers. They work with thousandths and begin using percentages.

**Year 6**:
Pupils simplify fractions and use common denominators. They multiply and divide fractions, convert between fractions, decimals and percentages fluently, and solve problems requiring rounding to specified accuracy.

## Measurement

**Year 3**:
Pupils measure length, mass and capacity using metric units. They calculate simple perimeters, work with money and read analogue clocks.

**Year 4**:
Pupils convert between metric units and calculate the perimeter and area of rectilinear shapes. They read and convert between analogue and digital time.

**Year 5**:
Pupils convert between metric units and approximate imperial equivalents. They calculate area of rectangles and estimate volume using cubic units.

**Year 6**:
Pupils calculate area of triangles and parallelograms and find volumes of cubes and cuboids. They convert units using decimals and apply formulae where appropriate.

## Geometry

### Properties of Shape

**Year 3**:
Pupils draw and identify 2D and 3D shapes. They recognise right angles and identify parallel and perpendicular lines.

**Year 4**:
Pupils classify triangles and quadrilaterals. They identify acute and obtuse angles and lines of symmetry.

**Year 5**:
Pupils measure and draw angles in degrees. They use angle rules for full turns and straight lines and distinguish between regular and irregular polygons.

**Year 6**:
Pupils draw shapes using given dimensions and angles. They calculate missing angles, understand circle parts, and recognise angle relationships including vertically opposite angles.

### Position & Direction

**Year 4**:
Pupils use coordinates in the first quadrant and describe translations.

**Year 5**:
Pupils describe and represent reflections and translations.

**Year 6**:
Pupils use all four quadrants and reflect and translate shapes on a coordinate grid.

## Statistics

**Year 3**:
Pupils interpret and present data using bar charts and pictograms and answer one- and two-step questions.

**Year 4**:
Pupils interpret discrete and continuous data, including time graphs.

**Year 5**:
Pupils read and interpret line graphs and timetables.

**Year 6**:
Pupils construct and interpret pie charts and calculate the mean average.

## Upper KS2 Additions

### Ratio & Proportion (**Year 6**):
Pupils solve problems involving scaling, percentages, similar shapes and unequal sharing.

### Algebra (**Year 6**):
Pupils use simple formulae, generate linear sequences, express missing number problems algebraically and solve equations with two unknowns.
